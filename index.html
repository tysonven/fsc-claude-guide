Step 2 of 7

Tell Claude who you are

Custom instructions are the single most powerful thing you can set up. They mean Claude always knows your business, your voice, and how you want to work — without you having to explain yourself every time.

How to set it up
It takes 5 minutes and saves you hours
  • Click your profile icon (top right) → SettingsThen go to "Profile" in the left menu
  • Fill in "What would you like Claude to know about you?"Use the template below as your starting point
  • Fill in "How would you like Claude to respond?"Tell it your preferred tone, format, and level of detail
Your starter template
Copy this and fill in the highlighted fields — make it yours
I run a [your business type, e.g. "business coaching practice / marketing consultancy / virtual assistant agency"] called [Business Name]. I work with [your ideal client, e.g. "small business owners in the retail sector"]. My main services are: [list 2–3 services] My tone of voice is: [e.g. "warm, direct, and practical — not corporate or overly formal"] Key things to know: - I'm based in [location] - I prefer [bullet points / prose / short answers] - When writing for clients, always match my tone: [friendly/professional/conversational] - I use [any tools, e.g. Notion, Google Workspace, HubSpot] in my business
✅ What good looks like

The more specific you are, the better Claude performs. "I'm a coach" is OK. "I'm a life coach for women returning to work after a career break, and I communicate warmly but professionally" is gold.

Step 3 of 7

Set up your first Projects

Projects are like dedicated workspaces inside Claude — each one remembers context, holds your files, and keeps work organised. Think of them as hiring different "versions" of Claude for different jobs.

How Projects work
From the sidebar, click "New Project" to create one
  • Click "+ New Project" in the left sidebarGive it a clear name so you know exactly what it's for
  • Add a Project instruction (custom prompt for this workspace)e.g. "This project is for writing client proposals. Always use formal language and include a pricing section."
  • Upload any relevant filese.g. your services brochure, brand guide, or a previous proposal as a template
Projects to create for your business
Start with 2–3, you can always add more

Step 4 of 7

Your business prompt toolkit

These are ready-to-use prompts tailored for coaches, consultants, and small business owners. Click any box to copy it — then paste straight into Claude.

Client Communication
Save hours on proposals, follow-ups, and onboarding
📄 Proposal Writer
Write a professional proposal for a new client. The client is [name/type], their problem is [brief description], and I'm proposing [service/solution]. Include an overview, what's included, timeline, and a section for investment/pricing. Match my business tone.
📧 Follow-Up Email
Write a warm follow-up email to a prospective client I met [time period] ago. We discussed [topic]. I want to check in, add value by sharing [brief insight or resource], and invite them to book a call. Keep it short — under 120 words.
Content Creation
Posts, newsletters, and ideas — on demand
📱 LinkedIn Post
Write a LinkedIn post about [topic/lesson/story from my business]. The post should feel personal and genuine, start with a hook line that stops the scroll, share a real insight, and end with a question or soft call to action. 150–200 words. My tone is [warm/direct/etc].
📰 Newsletter Section
Write a 200-word section for my email newsletter on the topic of [subject]. It should feel like I'm talking directly to my reader ([target client description]), be practical and specific, and end with one clear action they can take this week.
Systems & Operations
Build the processes that run your business
⚙️ SOP Creator
Create a step-by-step Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for [task, e.g. "onboarding a new client"]. Include: overview, who is responsible, step-by-step instructions, any tools or templates needed, and what success looks like. Format it so a new team member could follow it.
🔍 Strategy Researcher
I need a competitive analysis for my business. I offer [your services] to [your audience]. My main competitors appear to be [list 2–3 if you know them]. Analyse what they offer, how they position themselves, and identify 3 gaps or opportunities I could take advantage of.

Step 6 of 7

Connect Claude to your tools

This is where Claude stops being a chatbot and starts running parts of your business. Connectors link Claude directly to the apps you already use — so it can read your emails, update your calendar, search your files, and take actions without you switching tabs.

How to connect your first app
Takes less than 2 minutes per connector
  • Click the "+" button in the bottom-left of any chatOr type "/" to open the quick menu
  • Click "Connectors" to browse the directory50+ apps available — from Gmail to Notion to Zapier
  • Click "Connect" next to your chosen toolClaude redirects you to sign in — you approve exactly what it can access
  • Test it with a simple requeste.g. "Show me my latest Gmail messages" or "List my Notion pages from this week"
🔒 Is it safe?

Yes — Claude uses secure OAuth authentication. You always see exactly what Claude can access before approving, and you can disconnect any app at any time from Settings → Connectors.

Essential connectors for your business
Start with whichever tools you use most — connect one at a time
📧
Gmail + Google Calendar
Claude can search emails, summarise threads, draft replies, and check your calendar — all in one conversation. Ask: "Find all emails from [client] this month and summarise what they've asked for."
📁
Google Drive
Access, search, and work with your actual docs and files. Ask: "Find my Q4 proposal template in Drive and update it for a new client in the retail sector."
📓
Notion
Read and write to your Notion workspace. Ask: "Look at my client notes in Notion and draft a session agenda for tomorrow's call."
💬
Slack
Draft messages, summarise busy channels, and catch up on threads you missed. Ask: "Summarise the #clients channel from the last 2 days and flag anything urgent."
⚡
Zapier POWER MOVE
Connects Claude to 8,000+ apps. If there's no direct connector for a tool you use, Zapier bridges the gap. Build automations like: "When a new form is submitted → Claude writes a personalised reply → sends via Gmail."
5 workflows you can run today
Once your connectors are live, try these power combos
🌅 Morning Briefing (Gmail + Calendar)
Check my Gmail for anything urgent from the last 24 hours, then look at my calendar for today and tomorrow. Give me a summary of what needs my attention and suggest how to prioritise my morning.
📋 Client Prep (Notion + Calendar)
I have a call with [client name] at [time]. Find my notes about them in Notion, review what we last discussed, and create a short session agenda with 3 smart questions I should ask.
✉️ Inbox Zero Helper (Gmail)
Go through my last 20 unread emails. For each one, tell me: what it's about, whether it needs a reply, and if yes — draft a short response I can approve and send.
📊 Weekly Review (Notion + Gmail)
Look at my notes in Notion and any key emails from this week. Summarise: what I accomplished, what's still open, and help me write a clear plan for next week.
🗂️ Meeting Notes → Actions (Google Drive)
I've just finished a meeting. Here are my rough notes: [paste notes]. Clean these up, extract all action items with owners and deadlines, and save a tidy version to my Google Drive in the [folder name] folder.
💡 Note on connectors

Connectors are available on Pro, Max, Team, and Enterprise plans. Desktop-only connectors (for controlling apps on your computer) require the Claude desktop app. Web connectors work in your browser straightaway.
